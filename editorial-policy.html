Editorial Policy & Methodology

How we research, build, and maintain our financial calculators.

Our Editorial Mission

FreeMoneyIQ is committed to providing accurate, unbiased, and up-to-date financial information and calculation tools. Our editorial team follows a strict methodology to ensure every calculator, guide, and piece of content meets a high standard of factual accuracy and practical usefulness.

We do not accept payment from financial institutions, lenders, or any third party to influence our calculator results or editorial content. Our only revenue source is Google AdSense display advertising, which has no bearing on the tools we build or the information we present.

This policy was last reviewed and updated: July 2026

How Our Calculators Are Built

Every calculator on FreeMoneyIQ is built from verified financial formulas and cross-checked against authoritative government and industry sources before publication. Our development process follows these steps:

  • Formula verification: We implement standard financial formulas (amortization, compound interest, future value, etc.) and validate results against those produced by government agencies, academic finance tools, and established financial institutions.
  • Data sourcing: Rate inputs, tax brackets, fee schedules, and benchmark figures are sourced from official government publications and peer-reviewed industry surveys (see sources list below).
  • Independent testing: Each calculator is tested against a set of known inputs and expected outputs before publication.
  • Privacy review: All calculators are confirmed to operate entirely client-side, with no data transmitted to any server.

Primary Data Sources

Our financial calculators draw on the following authoritative sources:

  • IRS.gov — Federal income tax brackets, standard deductions, withholding tables (Publication 15-T), and self-employment tax rates used in our Tax Withholding Estimator and Freelancer Rate Calculator.
  • Federal Reserve (federalreserve.gov) — Benchmark interest rate data and historical mortgage rate context used across our loan and mortgage tools.
  • Freddie Mac Primary Mortgage Market Survey (PMMS) — Weekly 30-year and 15-year fixed mortgage rate benchmarks used in our Mortgage Calculator defaults.
  • Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) — Loan amortization standards, truth-in-lending disclosures, and debt calculation methodologies.
  • Social Security Administration (SSA.gov) — Contribution limits, payroll tax rates, and retirement planning benchmarks.
  • Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS.gov) — Wage data and self-employment statistics referenced in our Freelancer Rate Calculator context.

Content Update Schedule

Our editorial team reviews and updates all calculators and guides on the following schedule:

Annual Updates (minimum)

Every January, we review all calculators against newly published IRS tax tables, updated standard deductions, new Social Security contribution limits, and any relevant legislative changes from the prior year.

Quarterly Updates

We review benchmark rate data quarterly and update calculator defaults to reflect current market conditions, including prevailing mortgage rates and high-yield savings benchmarks.

Event-Driven Updates

When the Federal Reserve announces significant rate changes, when major tax legislation passes, or when government agencies publish new fee schedules, we update affected calculators promptly — typically within 2–4 weeks of the change taking effect.

All pages display a "Last updated" date to indicate when content was last reviewed.
